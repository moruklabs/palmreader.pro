The head line is not your IQ

April 24, 2026 · 4 min read

Half the palmistry content online treats the head line like a score. Long line, smart person. Short line, not so smart. That reading is both insulting and wrong. The head line is a description of how you think, not how much.

What the head line actually tracks

The head line runs across the palm below the heart line, starting near the thumb side. Traditional palmistry reads it as the signature of your mental style: where you start thinking, how far you are willing to carry a thought, and how you finish it. A straight line suggests linear, step-by-step reasoning. A curved line that drops toward the wrist suggests imaginative, associative thinking. Neither is better — they solve different problems.

A long head line is not a smart head line. It is a head line that does not stop early.

Length, curve, fork

  • Long and straight. The thought runs all the way through the plan before acting. Good for engineers, bad for improv.
  • Short and clean. Decisions get made quickly with what is in front of you. Good for operators, worse when the situation is ambiguous.
  • Curved downward. The thinker reaches for metaphor and association — writers, designers, researchers who follow a hunch.
  • Forked at the end (writer's fork). The ability to hold two framings at once. Useful in negotiation and craft, exhausting in daily life.

When the line joins the life line

If the head line starts attached to the life line, classical palmistry reads it as caution — decisions tied closely to family and safety. When it starts separately, the reading is independence, earlier risk-taking. This is not destiny. It is a report on a pattern that has already shaped early decisions. You can notice the pattern without being ruled by it.

How the app reads a head line

Palm Reader traces the head line from a photo and measures length, curvature, and whether it forks or splits. The interpretation the model writes back is never "your IQ is X." It is closer to "your line shows imaginative thinking that drops toward intuition — here is how that usually shows up in work and relationships." The line is data. The life around it is yours.
